This blog post introduces “Stop Guessing Perceptual Dimensionality: An Occam’s Razor for Ordinal Embeddings” (LORE). We explore how to jointly learn the intrinsic dimensionality and relative similarity structure from ordinal data, providing a principled way to build psychological spaces without prior assumptions about their dimensionality.

Introduction

How many dimensions are needed to represent the internal structure of a set of stimuli? This question has haunted psychological scaling since its inception. While methods like Multidimensional Scaling (MDS) allow us to build spaces, they often require the researcher to specify the dimensionality beforehand.

In this post, we discuss LORE (Learning Ordinal REpresentations), which uses an Occam’s razor approach to automatically determine the most appropriate dimensionality for a given dataset of ordinal comparisons.
